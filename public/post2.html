Exercises numbers 2 - 3

Exercise 2 Comments

What are comments? In programming comments are sentences that your complier doesn't read. In Ruby comments are made with a #. Everything after the # will not be read by the complier. Comments are used for programmers to understand your code better. Practice your commenting skills

Your program:


puts "Hello World!"

# Comments could say anything

# Comments could tell other programmers what you're working on

#Comments are neccessary

puts "Print me please"

#Comments are for internal use only


Type it and save it under rubyex2.rb Then run the ruby file on your terminal by typing

ruby rubyex2.rb

What your output should look like:

Assuming you did this correctly this should be your output:


Hello World!
Print me please


If your terminal isn't printing this, you did something wrong. Go back and check what you did wrong, fix it and move on to exercise three.

Exercise 3 Math , Numbers, & Symbols

This is the part most people fear, numbers, numbers, numbers, it really isn't that bad. I'll break it down so its easy for you.

So, what math symbols are mostly used in programming?
These:

+ plus

- minus

/ slash

* asterick

% percentage

> greater-than

< less-than

>= greater-than-equal

<= less-than-equal

Your program:


1 puts "I will now count my roosters and hens:"

2

3 puts "Hens", 25 + 30 / 6

4 puts "Roosters", 100 - 25 * 3 % 4

5

6 puts "Now I will count the eggs:"

7
8 puts 3 + 2 + 1 - 5 + 4 % 2 - 1 / 4 + 6

9

10 puts "Is it true that 3 + 2 = 5?"

11

12 puts 3 + 2 < 5 - 7

13

14 puts "What is 3 + 2?", 3 + 2

15 puts "What is 5 - 7?", 5 - 7

16

17 puts "Oh, that's why it's false."

18

19 puts "How about some more."

20

21 puts "Is it greater?", 5 > -2

22 puts "Is it greater or equal?", 5 >= -2

23 puts "Is it less or equal?", 5 <= -2

ruby rubyex3.rb
